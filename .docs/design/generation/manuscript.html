I · The problem

For a hundred years the grid had one job — to push power one way, from a few enormous plants outward to many small, patient homes. That century is over: load is up nineteen percent in five years, while almost no new transmission has been built since 2009. Every blackout, every curtailed wind farm, is a market it cannot serve.

II · The shift

Storage, sensors, and forecasting have turned the grid into a market that now clears every few seconds, not every few years. The winners will not own the most copper — they will own the layer that decides, moment to moment, where every electron should go.
